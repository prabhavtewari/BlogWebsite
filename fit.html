Quarantine Fitness

Gyms and fitness centers around the world are closed due to the COVID-19 pandemic.Trying to stay motivated to achieve your fitness goals can seem like a tough job at this time. Having a pantry stocked with tasty food that seems irresistible makes it even harder to keep a track of your diet.You may be surrounded by friends or family that might not support your goals or agree with your fitness regime. Owning a home gym at this time is a huge boon, but that isn't the case with most of us. Buying equipment is also out of the picture as e-commerce giants have restricted their catalogues to essential products.
Here are some tips to help you maintain your gains or at the very least reduce the damage done to them.

Calisthenics

Calisthenics is a form of exercise consisting of a variety of movements that exercise large muscle groups, such as running, standing, grasping, pushing, etc. These exercises are often performed rhythmically and with minimal equipment, as bodyweight exercises.

If you consider yourself a beginner or even intermediate then you can easily maintain and gain muscle with bodywieght exercises.The key like always is volume and variety.Push ups, squats and sit ups are the exercises that most think of when trying to train without equiment but there are many variations and types of exercises you can perform to optimise muscle growth. If standard pushups are too hard, try knee pushups. If they are too easy try decline or explosive pushups.

If you have to perform too many squats to reach failure try pistol squats. They may be hard to perform right off the the bat so follow the progressions to do them well. Beginners usually spend too much time training abs. If your goal is getting a six pack, it would make more sense to reduce your body fat level first.

It is important to set realisitic goals. Don't jumo right in and try to do 200 push ups a day, like everything, moderation is key.


Using Household Items to Train

It would be a bad idea to use a broom or mop as a barbell or using plastic chairs as inclined benches, but if you choose wisely, objects around you can be used as weights to have a better training session. You can fill a bag with heavy books and put it on to make your leg exercises more challenging. You can easily perform biceps curls with the bag. Hang on to the ledge of a loft to do pull ups and hanging leg raises, perform chest dips on the corner of the kitchen counter. Stuff a few newspapers under a door and put a towel on top of it to do pullups if the lofts aren't sturdy.


Intensity

Gym or no gym, may people lack intensity in their workouts. 3 sets of 12 is what is thrown around whenever you ask about the number of reps and sets while sets of 8-12 are optimal for muscle hypertrophy (growth) it has to be at an adequate weight. When you workout you should Use 50-75% of your one-repetition maximum (1RM) for 3-6 sets of 8-20 repetitions.

Put simply, you have to be putting in max effort by your last rep, be it the first or last set of your workout. To accurately describe this imagine the effort you would put in to lift a weight if someone were to put a gun to your head or offered you 5 crore Rupees, that's how you should feel while performing the last few reps of your set. You don't have much control over the weight in this situation so you should perform the maximum amount of reps you can if you want to really gain muscle. Try to push yourself to failure on every set. If your goal is to just lose fat you don't need the same intensity.

Cardio

Cardio is an extremely effictive way to increase calorie expenditure. It improves cardiovascular health and can help boost your mood. Yes, it really does. Your brain releases endorphines (hormones that make you feel good) when you do cardio.

Going out for a run or walk isn't an option but if you live in an apartment you can easily use the stairs. Going up and down the stairs at a moderate pace for 35-60 minutes 3-4 times a week is what you should aim for. If you want to make it harder you can put on the backpack filled with books.

High Intensity Interval Training (HIIT) is also an option. As the name suggests you do exercises like high knees,Jumping jacks and burpees at your best pace and then take a small break and repeat. If done properly, it can be effective. Dancing along to videos can also be considered.

Diet

Fitness is 80% diet and 20% exercise. It doesn't matter if you run for an hour a day only to go home and snack on namkeen and fried food.Calories in; Calories out are the basic rule you should follow. Being locked in your house means your energy expenditure is probably lower than it usually is as you are less active through the day. To compensate for this try to reduce your portions of carbohydrates and fats a bit.

It is hard to stay focused on your diet if you have snacks lying around the house. The best way to deal with this is to keep them out of sight to avoid getting tempted. Even elite athletes would want to eat junk is they saw them lying around their house. Stock up on low calorie foods with lots of fiber to fill you up just in case you get hungry. Some foods like meats might not be available at this time, eggs are easily available in most areas though. Egg whites are extremely satiating and high in protein, so eat them regularly. The yolks are a good source of healthy fats and contain protein too.

Sleep and Stress

These are the most underrated factors. Cortisol is the stress hormone and it reduces muscle growth, increases fat cell production and muscle loss.

When you sleep less, your body starts to burn calories at a slower rate to preserve energy.
